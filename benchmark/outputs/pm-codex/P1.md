## Part 5: Disruption Theory â€” Vectors and COAP Analysis

### Disruption Vector Assessment

**Is the marketing PM market over-served or under-served?**

The correct answer is: **under-served for marketing-specific jobs, over-served for generic task management jobs.**

Evidence:
- Marketing teams consistently report using Asana/Monday for basic task tracking while maintaining separate tools for briefs (Google Docs), assets (Drive/Dropbox), calendars (spreadsheets), and reporting (separate dashboards). This is the "I need multiple tools to do what one tool should do" signal â€” a classic under-served market [Tier 6: community discussions on r/marketing, Product Hunt comments for PM tools â€” hypothesis-grade, needs Tier 1 validation via interviews].
- At the same time, marketing teams often report not using 60-70% of Asana's features â€” the task management layer is more than good enough for basic coordination [Tier 6: anecdotal â€” needs validation].

**Disruption Vector for Your Startup:**

| Vector | Applicable? | Mechanism | Evidence Grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low-end disruption** | Partially | Enter at lower price, simpler feature set vs. Asana | Risky â€” Asana already has a free tier; ClickUp owns the price-disruption position |
| **New-market disruption** | Yes â€” primary vector | Serve marketing teams who are using spreadsheets + email + Drive as their "PM system" â€” non-consumption is real | Moderate [Tier 6: industry estimates suggest 40-60% of SMB marketing teams have no dedicated PM tool] |
| **Hybrid disruption** | Yes â€” ideal vector | Simpler than Asana for marketing-specific work AND accessible to teams who were never PM tool users | This is the Notion playbook â€” they did it for knowledge work; you do it for marketing |

**New-market disruption is the primary vector.** The real competitive set includes the team that coordinates campaigns in a shared Google Sheet and a Slack channel. These are not Asana customers you're taking â€” they are non-consumers you're creating a market for. This is a larger opportunity and a less defended position than trying to displace Asana's installed base.

### Conservation of Attractive Profits (COAP) Analysis

**Where are profits shifting in this value chain?**


**COAP finding:** As generic project management commoditizes (ClickUp, free tiers, bundled platform features), profits shift upward to **the layer that connects execution to outcomes** â€” marketing analytics integration, campaign performance reporting, and eventually AI-driven campaign intelligence. **Strategic implication:** Build toward marketing outcome integration now, even if it's lightweight, because this is where COAP says profits will concentrate. A startup that owns "the PM tool that shows you how your campaigns performed" has a durable profit position; one that only owns "the PM tool for marketing tasks" will face margin compression as the task management layer commoditizes.
