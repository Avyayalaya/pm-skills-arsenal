## Step 4: Positioning Analysis

### Current Positioning Statements (Inferred)

**Jumio:** For enterprise compliance and security teams who need maximum accuracy and regulatory defensibility, Jumio is the identity verification platform that provides the highest document coverage and forensics depth. Unlike point solutions, Jumio's global coverage and compliance pedigree reduces audit risk.

**Onfido:** For growth-stage and enterprise companies that need fast, global identity verification without friction, Onfido is the biometric identity platform that converts more legitimate users. Unlike legacy compliance tools, Onfido's AI-first approach balances security with customer experience.

**Persona:** For product and engineering teams building compliance into their product flows, Persona is the composable identity platform that lets you customize every verification step without writing infrastructure. Unlike rigid, sales-heavy platforms, Persona adapts to your exact business logic.

**Stripe Identity:** For businesses already using Stripe payments, Stripe Identity is the identity verification product that activates with one click. Unlike standalone vendors, Stripe Identity eliminates integration complexity entirely.

### Positioning Gaps and Opportunities

**Unclaimed positions — value propositions no competitor currently owns:**

1. **"Vertical-specific identity intelligence"** — No one owns the claim of "the identity platform purpose-built for [healthcare / insurance / regulated lending / background screening]." This is an open position if you have the capability.

2. **"The operator-friendly platform"** — Persona owns developer experience. Jumio owns compliance trust. Nobody clearly owns "the platform your ops and compliance team actually likes to use day-to-day."

3. **"Transparent accuracy benchmarks"** — Identity verification accuracy is a black box. A vendor who publishes third-party accuracy benchmarks, pass rates by document type, and false positive / false negative rates would differentiate meaningfully with sophisticated enterprise buyers.

**Crowded positions (avoid):**
- "AI-powered identity verification" — everyone says this; no one owns it
- "Frictionless user experience" — overused across the category
- "Enterprise-grade security" — table stakes, not differentiation

**Vulnerable positions competitors hold:**
- Jumio's "maximum coverage" claim is expensive and slow to iterate; a focused challenger with better speed-to-accuracy on a high-priority document set could beat them selectively
- Onfido's "biometric-first" position is under pressure as competitors (including Persona and new entrants) match liveness detection quality

## Step 5: Win/Loss Analysis Framework and Findings

*Note: The following is structured as the framework to apply immediately, plus the hypotheses most likely to be true based on market structure. You must validate with real interviews.*

Immediately conduct win/loss interviews for:

| Pattern | Hypothesis | If True: Implication |
|---|---|---|

Track quarterly. Flag any decline greater than 5 percentage points. The leading indicators matter more than the current rate.

## Step 6: Stripe Entry — Structured Assessment

### What Stripe Has

| Asset | Strength | Relevance to You |
|---|---|---|
| Distribution (Stripe customers) | Extraordinary — millions of businesses | High: many of your pipeline prospects are Stripe users |
| Brand trust | Very high in tech/startup segment | Moderate: enterprise buyers trust Stripe for payments, not yet for compliance |
| Engineering talent | World-class | High: they will improve the product quickly |
| Integration simplicity | Native for Stripe users | High for the segment of companies doing payment-adjacent verification |
| Regulatory / compliance depth | Currently low | Low short-term, watch long-term |
| Vertical coverage | Currently generic | Low short-term, watch as they acquire depth |

### What Stripe Doesn't Have (and Won't Have Quickly)

- Deep enterprise compliance workflow (manual review queues, audit trails, regulatory reporting)
- Regulated industry certifications and customer references (banking, healthcare, insurance)
- The relationship capital and professional services that enterprise procurement requires
- Cross-customer fraud network intelligence that compounds with scale
- Coverage depth for complex jurisdictions and document types

### Stripe's Actual Target (Be Precise About This)

Stripe Identity will win decisively in: high-growth tech companies, marketplace platforms, fintech companies already deep in the Stripe ecosystem, companies doing identity verification primarily in a payment context, buyers who prioritize speed of integration over depth of compliance.

Stripe Identity will NOT win quickly in: regulated financial institutions requiring BSA/AML depth, healthcare companies, insurance carriers, B2B companies doing employee or contractor verification, companies requiring regional document expertise outside the US/UK/EU.

**The critical question:** What percentage of your current 50 customers and your pipeline fall into Stripe's real target zone vs. the enterprise compliance zone where you are more defensible? This segmentation will tell you how much of a near-term vs. long-term problem Stripe represents.

## Step 7: Market Trends Identification

### Trend Analysis

**Trend 1: AI-native verification raising the accuracy baseline**
- What is changing: Foundation models and multimodal AI are dramatically compressing the gap between specialized ML systems and general-purpose approaches. The "data moat" that large incumbents relied on is shrinking.
- Why now: GPT-4 vision, Google Gemini, and purpose-built document understanding models are commoditizing baseline accuracy.
- Who is affected: All players; particularly threatens Jumio's ML accuracy differentiator.
- Timeline: Already happening; accelerates in 2025-2026.
- Implication for you: The accuracy gap that hurt you vs. Jumio is narrowing; invest in vertical-specific signals rather than general accuracy.
- Strategic response: **Fast follow** — use commodity AI infrastructure to close the accuracy gap without building from scratch; focus investment on the layer above (vertical intelligence, ops tooling, compliance workflow).

**Trend 2: Identity verification consolidating into platform bundles**
- What is changing: Authentication platforms (Okta, Auth0), payment platforms (Stripe), and fraud platforms (Sardine, Sift) are all adding identity verification as a component. Standalone verification is under bundling pressure.
- Why now: As verification quality commoditizes, buyers prefer consolidating vendors.
- Who is affected: All standalone verification vendors.
- Timeline: 12-36 month acceleration.
- Implication for you: Your standalone position is structurally challenged. You need to either become a platform (expensive) or go deep enough in a vertical that bundled solutions can't match your depth.
- Strategic response: **Vertical depth over horizontal breadth** — pick the vertical(s) where bundling doesn't work and standalone depth is required.

**Trend 3: Regulatory expansion (EU AI Act, UK Online Safety Act, US state biometric laws)**
- What is changing: Compliance requirements for biometric data collection are expanding and becoming more jurisdiction-specific.
- Who is affected: Everyone, but companies with less compliance infrastructure bear disproportionate burden.
- Timeline: Now through 2026-2027.
- Implication for you: Compliance investment is now a real investment requirement, but it also creates a barrier that advantages well-prepared vendors.
- Strategic response: **Lead in a specific regulatory context** — pick the regulatory environment where your target customers face the most acute need and build genuine compliance depth. This is a cornered resource play if done well.

**Trend 4: Fraud signal sharing networks**
- What is changing: Platforms that aggregate fraud signals across their entire customer base create compounding intelligence advantages. The more verifications processed, the better the fraud detection.
- Who is affected: Smaller vendors are disadvantaged vs. large-volume incumbents.
- Timeline: Already structural; compounds over time.
- Implication for you: At $8M ARR / 50 customers, your fraud network is limited. This is a genuine capability gap vs. Jumio and any Stripe-backed platform.
- Strategic response: **Partner or acquire** — consider partnerships with fraud intelligence providers (Sardine, Sift, Shield) to supplement your network with external data signals rather than building from scratch.

## Strategic Recommendations: Prioritized Action Plan

### Immediate (Next 30 Days)

**Priority 1: Conduct 10 win/loss interviews with recent buyers**
Do this yourself as Head of Product, not through your sales team. You need unfiltered customer truth. This is the highest-leverage activity for understanding your actual competitive position.

**Priority 2: Segment your 50 customers by vulnerability to Stripe**
Categorize each account: (a) payment-adjacent verification that Stripe could serve, (b) regulated industry compliance needs Stripe can't yet address. This segmentation tells you where to focus retention energy.

**Priority 3: Identify your single strongest vertical**
Look at your top 10 customers by ARR and NPS. There is almost certainly a vertical cluster — fintech KYC, insurance, healthcare, background screening, gig economy platforms. Name it explicitly.

### Short-Term (30-90 Days)

**Priority 4: Define a concrete differentiated positioning**
You cannot stay at "adequate across everything." Pick the specific buyer type, use case, and claim you will own. It should be defensible, falsifiable, and relevant to a buyer's actual decision. "The fastest enterprise identity deployment for regulated lenders" is a positioning. "Accurate, flexible, enterprise-ready" is not.

**Priority 5: Deepen integration at your top 20 accounts**
Proactively work with your top accounts to embed more deeply — custom risk rules, ops workflow integration, decision analytics they rely on. Create customer-created switching costs, not just integration stickiness.

**Priority 6: Build case studies and references for your strongest vertical**
Stripe and Jumio have brand. You win through references. A compliance officer at a mid-market insurance company is far more persuaded by a peer reference from a similar company than by any marketing claim you can make.

### Medium-Term (3-12 Months)

**Priority 7: Invest in the compliance workflow layer**
This is the moat that Stripe cannot buy quickly and Persona hasn't prioritized. Enterprise compliance teams need: review queues, escalation workflows, audit reports, decision explanation, regulatory reporting. If you build the best ops and compliance workflow in your vertical, you become embedded in their operations — not just their tech stack.

**Priority 8: Establish a partnership with a fraud intelligence network**
Rather than trying to out-accumulate data vs. Jumio or Stripe, partner to bring in complementary fraud signal data. This closes a capability gap without requiring years of volume accumulation.

**Priority 9: Invest in analyst and compliance community visibility**
Gartner Peer Insights, G2 in your category, ACAMS (financial crime compliance), and relevant vertical trade associations. When enterprise buyers short-list vendors, they search these sources. You need presence there before the conversation starts.
