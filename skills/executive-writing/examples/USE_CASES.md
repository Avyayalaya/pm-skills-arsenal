## Use Case 1: Strategy One-Pager for CEO Alignment

**Scenario:** A PM at a B2B SaaS company needs to get CEO alignment on whether to expand into enterprise or double down on mid-market. The CEO reads 40+ documents a week and skips anything that doesn't lead with the point.

### Before (Generic AI — ~250 words)

> **Enterprise Expansion Analysis**
>
> Our company has been experiencing strong growth in the mid-market segment with 40% YoY revenue growth. As we consider future growth vectors, there are several options to evaluate.
>
> Enterprise customers typically have higher ACVs and lower churn rates. However, entering the enterprise segment requires significant investment in compliance (SOC2, HIPAA), dedicated customer success, and enterprise sales capabilities. The mid-market segment continues to show strong demand but faces challenges with higher churn rates.
>
> We recommend considering a phased approach that balances both opportunities. This would allow us to maintain our mid-market momentum while gradually building enterprise capabilities.
>
> Key considerations include investment requirements, timeline to revenue, competitive dynamics, and organizational readiness.

### After (Skill-Powered — ~500 words)

> # Double Down on Mid-Market — Enterprise Is a 2027 Play, Not a 2026 Play
>
> **Date:** 2026-03-12 | **For:** CEO | **Ask type:** Alignment
>
> ## Situation
> DataVault is growing 40% YoY in mid-market analytics with strong product-market fit.
>
> ## Complication
> Enterprise prospects are reaching out unprompted — 3 inbound requests above $100K ACV in Q1. But mid-market churn at 4% monthly means we replace nearly half our customer base every year.
>
> ## Recommendation: Sequence mid-market first, enterprise second (H)
>
> **Option 1: Enterprise-first** — $6.5M investment, first revenue Q2 2027, payback 24-30 months.
> **Option 2: Mid-market acceleration** — $800K investment, revenue impact Q2 2026, payback 4-6 months.
> **Option 3 (Recommended): Sequence** — Mid-market now ($800K), SOC2 starts Q3 2026 ($2M). Enterprise sales hire Q1 2027 when certification is in hand.
>
> Key assumption: enterprise demand is real but not urgent — the 3 inbound requests are exploratory, not competitive-loss driven (H).
>
> **The Ask:** Align on Option 3 by this meeting. If aligned, we bring a detailed enterprise readiness plan to the June exec review. If not aligned, Q2 SDR hiring cycle is at risk.

### What Changed

- **Answer first:** Generic version builds to a vague recommendation. Skill version puts the recommendation in the title and first paragraph.
- **Structural options:** Generic version says "phased approach." Skill version presents 3 structurally different options with quantified costs, timelines, and payback periods.
- **Explicit Ask:** Generic version ends with "key considerations." Skill version ends with a specific alignment request, a deadline, and the cost of delay.
- **Audience calibration:** Skill version is framed for a CEO (strategic positioning) with a CFO callout (payback periods).
- **Confidence and assumptions:** Skill version states H/M/L confidence and names the key assumption that would change the recommendation.
