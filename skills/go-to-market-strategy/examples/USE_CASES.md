## Use Case 1: SaaS Product Launch — Collaborative Whiteboarding Tool

**Scenario:** Seed-stage startup launching a real-time collaborative whiteboarding tool with AI-generated templates. $2M funding, 4-person team, 6 months of runway. Competing against Miro, FigJam, Lucidspark.

**Before (generic AI, ~250 words):**
> You should target remote teams who need whiteboarding tools. Consider using Product Hunt for your launch, social media marketing, and content creation about remote collaboration. Price competitively against Miro at around $5-8/user/month. Launch with a free tier to get adoption, then convert to paid. Track metrics like signups, active users, and revenue. The whiteboarding market is large and growing, especially with remote work trends.

**After (skill-powered, ~500 words):**
> **Market Entry Thesis:** The wedge is Miro's free tier cliff — teams hitting the 3-board limit during sprint planning who can't justify $8-16/user/month for a tool used 4-6 hours/week. LLM cost reduction (85% in 12 months) makes AI template generation viable at $5/user/month — a cost structure impossible 18 months ago (H).
>
> **Beachhead:** Remote product teams at 20-100 employee companies using Miro free tier. ICP score: 4.3/5.0 (validated via 8 customer interviews, T2). Can name 100+ prospects via Slack community directories and ProductHunt upvoter lists.
>
> **Primary Channel:** PLG via Product Hunt launch → Slack app directory. Estimated CAC: $16.67 (T6). Payback: 0.7 months. LTV/CAC: 21.6:1 [EVIDENCE-LIMITED: all T6 estimates, replace with actuals from beta].
>
> **Launch Sequence:** 4-week closed beta (15 design partners) → 4-week open beta (Product Hunt) → GA. Gate: ≥10/15 partners using weekly by Week 3. Kill: <30 signups from PH launch → pivot channel.
>
> **Failure Criteria:** CAC >$300 after 50 conversions = STOP. Miro announces AI templates = PIVOT positioning immediately.
>
> **Biggest weakness:** Miro adds AI templates within 6 months, neutralizing the wedge before switching costs accrue (M).

**What Changed:**
- Generic output says "target remote teams" — skill output defines a specific segment scoreable by ICP criteria (4.3/5.0)
- Generic output lists channels — skill output calculates unit economics per channel with evidence tiers
- Generic output has no failure criteria — skill output defines SMART-K kill conditions
- Generic output assumes success — skill output names the biggest risk upfront and pre-plans the pivot
- Generic output is ~250 words of common knowledge — skill output is ~500 words of uncommon knowledge (Miro free tier cliff as wedge, LLM cost curve as timing thesis)
